Project Overview

The Diamond Jubilee Hall Renovation

The Diamond Jubilee Hall stands as one of the most iconic spaces on the CET campus — a venue that has hosted generations of students, faculty, and distinguished guests across convocations, cultural events, and landmark gatherings.

The proposed renovation will modernise the hall with full air-conditioning, upgraded acoustics, improved lighting, and a complete overhaul of ancillary spaces including restrooms, lobbies, and accessibility ramps.

This is not merely a renovation — it is a commitment to the generations of CETians yet to come, and a tribute to all who walked those halls before.

Project Objectives

What This Renovation
Will Achieve

01
Full Air-Conditioning
Installation of a modern, energy-efficient centralised air-conditioning system to ensure year-round comfort for all occupants and events.
02
Acoustic Upgrades
Improved soundproofing and acoustic panels to transform the hall into a world-class venue for seminars, cultural programmes, and convocations.
03
Modern Lighting
State-of-the-art LED lighting systems with programmable ambience controls, replacing ageing fixtures throughout the main hall and ancillary areas.
04
Ancillary Spaces
Complete renovation of lobbies, washrooms, backstage areas, and storage facilities to meet contemporary standards of hygiene and function.
05
Accessibility
Installation of ramps, accessible seating zones, and inclusive facilities ensuring the hall is welcoming to all members of the community.
06
Structural Restoration
Essential structural repairs and façade restoration to preserve the architectural heritage of this beloved campus landmark for future generations.
